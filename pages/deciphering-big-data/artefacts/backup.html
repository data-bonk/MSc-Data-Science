Grandfather-Father-Son (GFS) Backup Strategy

The Grandfather-Father-Son (GFS) backup strategy organises data backups into three generations: daily (Son), weekly (Father), and monthly (Grandfather), aiming to minimise storage space and maximise recovery points (Gibraltar Solutions, 2023). While GFS reduces resource overhead through hierarchical retention policies, making it superior to simple full backup strategies, its effectiveness for large databases requires critical evaluation.

GFS maximises recovery points by utilising minimal storage, automatically purging older backups according to predefined schedules (Gibraltar Solutions, 2023). However, modern incremental backup methods prove more resource-efficient for high-change environments, transferring unique data blocks since the last backup. For big data applications that require frequent updates, as emphasised in McKinney's (2022) data analysis frameworks, cloud-native snapshot technologies and continuous data protection offer more granular recovery points with lower operational overhead.

GFS's effectiveness diminishes in high-velocity data environments where differential backups offer superior restoration speed (Gibraltar Solutions, 2023). While GFS remains reliable for regulation-compliant backup strategies, it functions best as a complementary rather than primary solution for large databases, where modern incremental and synthetic approaches deliver superior performance and resource optimisation for complex data wrangling scenarios (McKinney, 2022).
